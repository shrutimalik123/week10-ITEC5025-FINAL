CATEGORY 11 – MEDICAL TERMS & CLINICAL KNOWLEDGE (Q88–Q94)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q88: What does ALT/SGPT measure?
A:   ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase), also called SGPT, is a liver enzyme
     measured in blood. Elevated levels can indicate liver damage or disease.
     Normal range is approximately 7–56 U/L. The sample data shows a patient
     value of 40.0 U/L, which is within the normal range.

Q89: What is RDW in a CBC?
A:   RDW (Red Cell Distribution Width) measures the variation in size of red
     blood cells. Normal RDW is approximately 11.5–14.5%. A value of 15.0%
     (as seen in the sample data) is mildly elevated and may indicate iron
     deficiency anemia or mixed nutrient deficiency.

Q90: What does low albumin mean?
A:   Low serum albumin (hypoalbuminemia) — below 3.4 g/dL — can indicate
     malnutrition, liver disease (cirrhosis), kidney disease (nephrotic
     syndrome), or inflammatory conditions. The sample patient showed albumin
     of 2.8 gm/dL, which is below the normal range.

Q91: What is ICD-10?
A:   ICD-10 (International Classification of Diseases, 10th Revision) is a
     global standard for coding diseases, symptoms, procedures, and causes of
     death. Each diagnosis in the database is coded with an ICD-10 code (e.g.,
     F80.1 = Expressive language disorder, I36 = Nonrheumatic tricuspid valve
     disorders).

Q92: What is monocytes in a CBC?
A:   Monocytes are a type of white blood cell involved in immune response.
     In a CBC (Complete Blood Count), monocytes are measured in thousands per
     cubic millimeter (k/cumm). Normal range is approximately 0.2–1.0 k/cumm.
     The sample patient shows 0.9 k/cumm, within the normal range.

Q93: medical terms
A:   I can explain clinical terms when you ask about them directly. For example:
     'What does ALT mean?' / 'What is RDW?' / 'Explain ICD-10'
     Use the 'ner' command to extract medical entity names from clinical text.

Q94: What does poverty_pct mean in the patient data?
A:   The poverty_pct field represents the percentage of the residential
     population in that patient's geographic area that is below the federal
     poverty line. It is a socioeconomic indicator, not individual income data.
